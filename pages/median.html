Making use of median in real life

Scenario

In this example, you want to figure out the average money of a student in their wallet in your classroom.
You might be tempted to use the mean to get the average, however what if one student carries a lot of money.

money (in US dollars) = [100, 150, 90, 87, 110, 10000]
Now, let's get the average using the mean.
Heads up, this will be incredibly inaccurate!
100 + 150 + 90 + 87 + 110 + 10000 = 10,537
10,537 / 6 = $1756.17
So, in this case we use median instead of mean.

How to get median

  1. Sort the values into ascending order.
    87, 90, 100, 110, 150, 10000
  2. Get the middle value, in this case it's values.
    100 and 110. Add the two middle values and divide it by two.
    You get $105.0 which makes sense than $1756.17 right?

Relevance to data set

Mean Median
The mean is not a robust tool...
since it is largely influenced by outliers.		 The median is better suited for skewed distributions to derive at central tendency...
since it is much more robust and sensible.
